An original crust large cheese pizza costs $10.99. To figure out the number of toppings I can get, I see that I have \(\$25 - \$10.99 = \$14.01\) to spend on toppings. Each topping costs $1.50. (Parts a, b, and c are different possible ways to proceed from here.)

  1. I can find the number of toppings I can afford by dividing $14.01 by $1.50. This gives me 9.34. The pizza restaurant will only allow me to buy whole numbers of toppings so I can get 9 toppings on a $25 large original crust pizza.

  2. If I start with $14.01 and subtract 1.50 until I can't subtract it anymore, I can do that 9 times, so I can afford 9 toppings on my pizza if I have $25 to spend.
